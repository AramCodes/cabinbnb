Frequently Asked Questions

CabinBnB is similar to our competitor AirBnB but only for cabins. CabinBnB caters to both owners who want to rent their vacation property and to guests who are looking to rent great accommodations.This is a great place to list your home if you are looking to easily rent your home or spare room to travelers.

The “How to Host” FAQ section will be specific to your search location. For example, if you are viewing this information from Canada then the information that CabinBnB provides you with will be based on your region. When you arrive on the landing pages for hosts, it will provide you with all the information you need to know about hosting and it will allow you to register as a host. All of the details that you are going to need are collected here. It is easy for you to get started and to learn the basics, such as how money is handled in regards to the payments for your property. You have access to this information at all times when you are using CabinBnB to rent your property.

CabinBnB is similar to our competitor AirBnB but only for cabins. In most case our host have electronic locks on their door or the key will be locked in a box with an electronic lock. Electrical locks can be accessed via a code that will be e-mailed to your itinerary the day of your booking. If the property does not have a box the host will bring you the key or leave the key somewhere and text you where the day of your booking.

Your plans have changed and now you need to cancel your reservation. No problem. You can go to your Trips to cancel or make changes to your reservation. Or if you’re experiencing an issue during your stay, you can ask your Host to fix it, request a partial refund, or request to cancel your reservation for a full refund. It’s important to submit your request within 24 hours of noticing the issue, and your Host will have 1 hour to respond. If they decline or don’t respond, you can ask CabinBnB to step in to help.

Most Hosts pay a flat service fee of 3% of the booking subtotal. The subtotal is your nightly price plus any optional fees you charge guests, like a cleaning fee, and doesn’t include CabinBnB fees and taxes. Guests typically pay a service fee of around 14% of the booking subtotal.

LandCover for Hosts is top-to-bottom protection for every Host on CabinBnB. LandCover for Hosts includes $1 million USD in Host liability insurance and $3 million USD in Host damage protection, with coverage for art, valuables, parked cars, boats, and other vehicles, and more.

CabinBnB Host Clubs are communities of local Hosts who connect online and in person to ask questions, share tips, celebrate accomplishments, and discuss what it’s really like to host.

Groups are led by volunteer Hosts called Community Leaders, who start discussions and organize meetups and volunteer opportunities. Leaders also partner with CabinBnB to share the latest updates and educational content to keep club members up to date.
